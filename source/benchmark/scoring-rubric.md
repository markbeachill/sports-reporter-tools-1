# Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to evaluate individual tool runs. Scores are guides, not a substitute for judgement.

## 1. Form recognition

Does the tool correctly identify the kind of article and judge it by the right standard?

- **0** — Misidentifies the form or uses the wrong criteria.
- **1** — Partly recognises the form but imports inappropriate expectations.
- **2** — Correctly recognises the form and applies mostly suitable criteria.
- **3** — Shows strong form awareness and adapts the analysis precisely.

## 2. Reader-journey insight

Does the tool explain what the piece does to the reader over time?

- **0** — Gives generic writing advice.
- **1** — Mentions reader effect but vaguely.
- **2** — Identifies a plausible reader journey.
- **3** — Gives a sharp, text-grounded account of reader movement, orientation, attachment and payoff.

## 3. Angle and attachment diagnosis

Does the tool identify what the reader is invited to care about?

- **0** — Reduces the article to topic or score.
- **1** — Notices the angle but not its emotional or narrative function.
- **2** — Identifies the main attachment route.
- **3** — Clearly distinguishes importance, liveness, attachment and reader stake.

## 4. Economy and voice

Does the tool judge the article’s phrase-level spending intelligently?

- **0** — Ignores language choices or gives generic style advice.
- **1** — Spots some weak phrasing but treats all issues equally.
- **2** — Identifies meaningful examples of waste, cliché or live detail.
- **3** — Distinguishes harmless cliché from load-bearing cliché and links phrase choices to reader effect.

## 5. Textual grounding

Does the tool stay anchored in the supplied article?

- **0** — Invents or relies heavily on unsupported facts.
- **1** — Some claims are grounded, but overreach is visible.
- **2** — Mostly grounded, with minor inference issues.
- **3** — Consistently grounded and clear about uncertainty.

## 6. Pedagogical usefulness

Would the output help the intended writer improve?

- **0** — Confusing, overwhelming or not actionable.
- **1** — Contains useful observations but poor prioritisation.
- **2** — Gives usable guidance matched to the writer level.
- **3** — Teaches a clear craft move the writer could apply immediately.

## 7. Prompt discipline

Does the tool obey its own declared limits?

- **0** — Rewrites when it should analyse, invents facts, or ignores the tool brief.
- **1** — Mostly follows the brief but slips into rewriting or unsupported advice.
- **2** — Follows the brief with minor drift.
- **3** — Fully respects the tool role and boundaries.

## Interpreting total scores

- **0–7** — Poor run. Do not rely on the output.
- **8–13** — Mixed run. Useful findings may be present but require careful human review.
- **14–18** — Good run. The tool is mostly behaving as intended.
- **19–21** — Strong run. The tool is producing expert-aligned, grounded and teachable analysis.
